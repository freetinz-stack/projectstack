The real cost

Medical costs hit hardest
when you're least prepared.

01

The deductible you forgot you had

January rolls around and your deductible resets. That prescription that cost $15 in December is $65 in January. You knew it was coming and still didn't plan for it.

02

Dental work that derails everything

A single crown: $1,200. Insurance covers 50%. You owe $600 in one go. Without a healthcare buffer in your budget, that comes straight from next month's rent money.

03

Out-of-pocket creep adds up silently

$25 copay here, $40 prescription there, $95 for that specialist visit your plan barely covered. Three months of "small" healthcare costs can easily total $400+.
