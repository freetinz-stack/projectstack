The traps most people don't see

Your debt isn't shrinking
as fast as you think it is.

01

The minimum payment trap

The minimum payment is designed to keep you paying as long as possible. It covers the interest and almost nothing else. Banks profit. You tread water.

02

Interest eating 40% of your payment

In the early years of most loans, less than half your payment goes toward the actual balance. The rest is interest. You'd be shocked to see the split in black and white.

03

No end in sight

Without a payoff date on the wall, debt feels permanent. You're making payments but there's no finish line — so there's no motivation to do anything differently.

Payoff strategies

Avalanche or snowball.
See which wins for you.

Debt Avalanche

Attack the highest interest first

Pay minimums on all debts except the one with the highest interest rate — throw everything you have at that one. When it's gone, roll that payment to the next highest. Mathematically optimal: saves the most money in total interest paid.

Debt Snowball

Attack the smallest balance first

Pay minimums on all debts except the smallest balance — pay that off first for a quick win. When it's gone, roll that payment to the next smallest. Psychologically powerful: builds momentum and motivation with early victories.

FincWin models both strategies with your actual debt data. You'll see the payoff date and total interest for each. The right choice depends on your personality and your numbers — we show you both so you can decide.

DTI Ratio

The number lenders
look at first.

Your debt-to-income ratio is total monthly debt payments divided by gross monthly income. Lenders use it to decide whether to approve a mortgage, car loan, or credit card. Most lenders want it below 36%. Above 43% and doors close.

FincWin calculates your DTI automatically and shows it on your dashboard. Know where you stand — before a lender does.
