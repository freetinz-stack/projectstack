The real cost

Entertainment spending is the easiest
category to lose track of.

01

The "it's only $20" that happens every weekend

Movie tickets: $22. Popcorn: $14. Parking: $8. That's $44 for one outing. Do that twice a month and add a concert, and your entertainment budget is $200+ before you blink.

02

Gaming purchases that sneak up on you

$60 game here. $15 DLC there. $50 for in-app purchases your kid made. Gaming can run $100–$200/month without feeling like it — every purchase felt small and justified in the moment.

03

Impulsive event tickets that break the budget

You spot a concert on sale and grab two tickets for $180. It felt like a treat. But it came on top of a week that already had two other outings. The Entertainment envelope would have caught it.
