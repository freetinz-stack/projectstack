The uncomfortable truth

Wealth isn't earned.
It's kept.

Most people who build real wealth don't earn dramatically more than their peers. They just leak dramatically less.

The leak is silent. It's the subscriptions that auto-renew. The dining budget that's $200 over every month. The car payment eating 18% of take-home. The interest on a loan that could be cleared with a modest extra payment. Individually, none of it feels fatal. Together, it silently consumes the gap between your income and your future.

FincWin doesn't make you earn more. It helps you see exactly where the leaks are. Once you see them, you can fix them. And the money you stop leaking doesn't just sit there — it becomes the surplus that, invested consistently, compounds into something that matters.

Net worth

Assets minus liabilities.
The number that matters.

Income is a flow. Net worth is a position. Your salary tells you how fast water is running into the bucket. Net worth tells you how much is actually in it. Most people can tell you their salary to the dollar and can't tell you their net worth to within $50,000.

FincWin tracks assets and liabilities together, so you always know the position. Not just what you earn — what you own, what you owe, and the gap between them.

Savings rate

The single metric
wealthy people obsess over.

High earners who save 5% build less wealth than moderate earners who save 25%. The savings rate — what percentage of your income you actually keep — determines how fast you move. Everything else is noise.

FincWin calculates your savings rate in real time. Not as a goal you set and forget — as a live measurement of whether this month's spending is building your future or consuming it.
